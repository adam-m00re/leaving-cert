Climate Change

What can we do to stop Climate Change?

 

We can't stop climate change, but we can prevent it getting much worse. The best thing we can all do is to put pressure on the government to act urgently. We can also make changes in our everyday lives to reduce our carbon emissions.

  1. Ask the government to continue climate action.

    2. Tackling climate emergency must take centre stage in the government’s plans. We need to urge our leaders to build climate friendly communities across the country – with warm homes, clean air, green jobs and thriving wildlife.

  2. Take action in your community

    3. Climate Action groups are the local solution to a global crisis. Right now people just like you are coming together to develop practical, local solutions and make their towns and cities more climate-friendly.

  3. Power up on plants

    4. Meat and dairy production is responsible for 14% of global climate-changing emissions.

    Too much meat is not good for your health either. Each year we could prevent thousands of early deaths and save the health system a fortune, by eating low-meat or no-meat diets.

  4. Top up you fitness

    5. Petrol and diesel cars spew out lots of climate-wrecking emissions. They also increase air pollution. Try and leave you car at home and top your fitness by cycling or walking. Too tired or too far to go, hop on a bus or train.

  5. Get your electricity from the wind and the sun

    6. Switch to a green-energy provider, without any disruption to your supply. Every person choosing a green tariff adds to the movement for more renewables.

  6. Take the train not the plane

    7. Aviation’s carbon footprint has grown by 20 per cent in Europe since 2005 and it’s increasing at an average of four per cent annually. The aviation sector accounted for around seven per cent of the UK’s total emissions in 2017 and is projected to be the single biggest source of emissions in the UK by 2050.It’s the well-educated middle classes that are responsible for that growth and who are feeling the ‘flight shame’ of their inequitable carbon footprint.

    The good news is that there are alternatives for most holidays in Europe i.e. travelling by train. It is possible to travel direct from London to the French Alps by Eurostar during winter weekends, or travel via Paris, connecting to a TGV to open up many more destinations.

  7. Save energy at home

    8. You can save energy at home by turning off lights and unplugging appliances. Insulating your home properly, use smart controls and heat pumps where you can will make it more Eco friendly.
